Porches

So far we have four bids and I have spoken to three representatives:

  • Mass Roofing and Gutters

  • Home & Garden

  • New England Vinyl Pro

1 Mass and Roofing

Ed Desantis is a sales rep for Mass Roofing and Gutters. He came in a suit and eye-balled the project. Don McDermott, his boss, had proposed "cedar impressions" on the phone and Ed very much pushed those as maintenance-free alternatives to wood.

He did not discuss alternatives but explained (roughly) that they would place tyvek on top of the existing wood, fasten cedar impressions on top, and use aluminum to cover the rotting window sills.

docx pdf

The total for this bid is $14.6K.

2 Home and Garden

JJ Haddad from Home & Garden Construction is a general contractor. He was clearly knowledgeable of the work that had to be done, and after inspecting the wood on the porches, he proposed two alternatives:

  • repair and repaint the porch. It is the least expensive alternative, with an estimated cost of $7500.

  • bid for something comparable to Mass Roofing and Cutters. However, it became clear that JJ did not know and had not worked with cedar impressions before. He was more comfortable with Vinyl and wood.

JJ pointed out that we definitely wanted shingles instead of horizontal boards because the siding and the porches are rather uneven and this would show very much with clapboards.

docx pdf

The total for this bid is $15.5K.

3 New England Vinyl Prof

Jimmy Nichols (recommended by our neighbor) has run New England Vinyl Pro for over 30 years; he says his company was one of the first to offer vinyl sidings and named his company accordingly. (This company is really just a subsidiary of his .)

Jimmy came out with a ladder during the week, inspected all three levels of our siding, and was quite direct with his explanations:

  • Our porches are obviously outdoor porches and, as such, will never be waterproof per se.

  • Putting cedar impressions on top of the existing siding is cosmetic and will slow down the water, but it won’t prevent it. He showed me the so-called "weeping holes" of the Vinyly siding of our neighbors.

  • He proposed to take off the existing siding instead of hiding it. He will reinforce the existing "nailers" (thin boards to which our siding is nailed to) with 2x4s and then nail plywood on top. (This is called a subwall.) Like the others, he will then insulate with Tyvek and put cedar impressions on top of this.

plain text

Note that Jimmy’s bid provides two numbers: $18K for the extensive work and $15K for the work that is comparable to the two others.

5 Summary

Jimmy’s explanation makes the most sense to me if (and that’s the critical condition) we wish to truly improve the porches. If we merely wish to improve the looks of our outdoor porches, any of the bids will do. Indeed, we could just repair and repaint them.
