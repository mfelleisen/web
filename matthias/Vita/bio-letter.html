For the past 25 years, Matthias Felleisen has conducted research on all aspects of programming languages; for the past 15 years he has also run a nation-wide outreach project for high schools and middle schools.

For his research on programming languages, Felleisen was inducted as an ACM Fellow in 2006 and received ACM SIGPLAN's Achievement Award in 2012. Also in 2012, ICFP named his ICFP 2002 paper on software contracts for higher-order languages the most influential publication.

For his educational outreach, ACM honored Felleisen with the Karl V. Karlstrom Award in 2010. ACM SIGCSE named Felleisen Outstanding Educator of the Year in 2011.

Felleisen has delivered keynote addresses at many major programming language conferences, including POPL, ECOOP, ICFP as well as the Symposium on Computer Science Education.

Matthias Felleisen obtained his PhD from Indiana University, Bloomington in 1987, a Diplom in Wirtschaftsingenieurswesen from Technische Hochschule Karlsruhe (Germany) in 1984, and an M.S. in Computer Science from the University of Arizona at Tucson in 1981.
