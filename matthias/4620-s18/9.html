9 — Capstone Memo

Due Thursday, 17 April; 9:00am

Capstone If you are enrolled as a "capstone student" (everyone who is not a freshman), please write a one-page memo reflecting on the following questions:

  • This course is a research course tailored to the undergraduate level. Such courses introduce students to a research topic that a research professor and his team are currently pursuing. For practical topics, this may expose you to ideas that will emerge in industrial settings five or ten years down the road.

    In this day and age, such courses are usually reserved for PhD students; universities tend to focus instead on "schooling courses" at the undergraduate level, which cover well-known and well-worked-out material.

    Do you think that Northeastern’s CCIS should offer such courses to undergraduates?

  • I have tried to offer the course in such a way that you have chance to experiment with presenting challenging, novel material to peers. I consider this skill critical for software developers who have ambitions beyond becoming a "coder."

    Do you think research courses should come with such elements or should they just focus on the research topic?

  • I have proposed to create a research track within the CCIS CS major. The CCIS Undergraduate Dean has planned it out with the Provost and is about to get final approval. It would base a series of research courses on Accelerated I and will likely allow research-track students to opt out of Fundamentals II/OOD or other courses.

    If given a chance, would you have pursued such a track? Why? Why not?

Delivery Please send me a PDF file named capstone-lastname.pdf.
