2 Grading Process

You must complete the grading within five days---Monday night for the Thursday morning deadline---of the due date for all project milestones.

All rubrics and self-evaluation forms will be available via the course repo. The instructors and pod leaders have access to the repo.

Grading takes place in a staff-only git repository:

    github.ccs.neu.edu/CS4500-F21/code-inspection

Cameron will create inspection forms in sub-directories of this repository, as specified below. You will fill out the inspection forms and push them back to origin.

  1. Soon after the assignment deadline, Cameron will push inspection forms to the shared repo. You can begin grading designs at this point, but not self-evaluations.

  2. On the evening after the assignment is due, Cameron will push a self-evaluation sheet into each pair’s repo. The pair has 24 hours to return answers. The goal is to get them to reflect on code and view it from the perspective of a future reader/maintainer.

    The self-evaluation form will come with a URL to the commit that we harvested as their final solution for an assignment.

  3. After the self-evaluation deadline, Cameron will update the inspection forms with links to all valid self-evaluations. (These will arrive by 10pm Saturday after a Friday morning deadline.)

    Cameron will check that all code URLs added by the students point to the commit URL sent out with the self-evaluation. If not, he will reject the entire self-evaluation and you will not find it in your grading repo.

  4. If we are grading assignment <A> and your pod leader’s Khoury name is <POD> and your Khoury username is <YOU>, then the to-be-graded code and self-evaluation plus the forms to be filled in will show up in

        github.ccs.neu.edu/CS4500-F20/code-inspection/<A>/<POD>/<YOU>/

    This directory will be populated with one inspection form per pair for the homework solution (<A>). See below for the shape of the form file and how to use it.

  5. Once homework solutions are assigned, the pod leaders will meet with their pod and jointly grade the repos assigned to the leader. The goal is for the pod to develop a common interpretation of the rubric. You may also use Slack to coordinate.

    If necessary, the pod leaders will communicate with MF to resolve ambiguities.

  6. As you inspect code and articulate feedback, the goal is to simulate this future reader for the pair. Critique their code base from the perspective of "I do not understand." Note Do not tell them how to fix it.

    For basically each code-inspection issue, they are expected to supply URLs to corresponding line ranges in github files. Hopefully this will also simplify your task and make it possible to get the grading done on time, even for large assignments.

    The pair may fail in various ways:

    if they

       

    we give

    fail to turn in a self-evaluation

       

    grade each code-inspection on a 60% scale.

    turn in a self-evaluation with bad links

       

    take off the accuracy points.

    explicitly say that they didn’t address an issue,

       

    given them 50% for this issue.

    misdirect for an issue,

       

    deduct half the accuracy points

    misdirect for two issues,

       

    they lose all accuracy points.

  7. For every pair, there will be a form file in a the pair’s directory. The form file has this format:

    Here is the format for a code-inspection report:

        Pair: <pair-name>

        Commit: <commit-hash>

        Self-eval: <URL to the self-evaluation file>

        Score: <points-earned>/<total>

        Grader: <name>

        

        free-text

    You will fill in the grade, write free-form notes in the free-text area, and push them back to origin as you make progress.

  8. When all graders of a pod are done, the pod leader will spot-check the grading and inform Cameron when the task is completed.

  9. Cameron will push the feedback up to the students.

Graders should bring up any difficulties with their pod leaders first (interpreting the rubric, grading a specific case, meeting with a pair, or being late with grading).
