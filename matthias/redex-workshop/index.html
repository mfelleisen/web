Redex Summer School

Matthias Felleisen, Robert Bruce Findler, Matthew Flatt


with David Van Horn (University of Maryland)

27–31 July 2015

The table below presents a concise workshop schedule. With the exception of Monday and Friday morning, every session will consist of a lecture part (60 – 90 minutes) and a hands-on lab (120-180 minutes). The lectures will introduce basic Redex ideas; the labs will present practical exercises on these topics. We will have a break between lectures and labs, and we expect you to take breaks on your own during lab time.

Monday morning will present the theoretical framework of reduction semantics using the lambda calculus as an example. While we expect that participants have some familiarity with the latter, we will present our perspective on this idea, solidifying literature from 1975 through now.

Friday morning is your turn. We hope that you come to Utah with a modeling project in mind, and we are ready to help you getting started.
