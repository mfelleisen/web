How I Write Signatures in Untyped CodeðŸ”—

#;  {[*Equations X] [Bag X] [Bag X] -> [Equations X]}
#;  (useful left-to-right my-wallet bank)
;;  return those equations ‘e‘ in ‘left-to-right‘ for which ‘my-wallet‘ has
;;  enough Xs to swap one side and ‘bank‘has enough Xs for the other;
;;  orient the resulting equations so that ‘my-wallet‘ covers the left
 
(define (useful left-to-right my-wallet bank)
  (define right-to-left (map 1eq-flip left-to-right))
  (for/fold ([result '()]) ([e (append left-to-right right-to-left)]
             #:when (can-swap? e my-wallet bank))
    (cons e result)))

In Racket #; turns the next S-expression into a comment and and ;; turns the rest of the line into a comment.

How I Factored the Required FunctionðŸ”—

Recognize the above function as a composite of:

  • 1eq-flip, which flips the side of an equation

  • can-swap?, which checks whether the left-to-right direction of an equation can be used to trade.

The composition uses two loops (map and for/fold) and one built-in (append).
