3 Simulating MaintenanceðŸ”—

It means learning to read and analyze a problem statement; teasing out the essential aspects; describing the corresponding software components; and making a plan of how to build them. Once this plan exists and is understood, they must acquire the discipline to stick to the plan and to consult it again and again as they construct the software.

Some time during the semester students must rotate from one code base to another. It is the only way for them to understand what maintenance feels like—even just building atop an existing body of software—why it is critical to help the future maintainer as much as possible. Or, as the book’s title says, to act in a socially responsible manner.
