4 Code Walk Process

Each section TA is assigned a "shoulder" and three graders for the duration of the semester:

The head of each section will assist the instructor with conducting code walks: managing questions from on-line participants, observing the presenters and the panelists, making suggestions, and correcting the instructor (if needed).

The shoulder attends too and "shoulders" some of the work of the head, especially observing the presenters and the panelists. It might be best to take notes. After the lecture, the instructor, the section head, and shoulder meet and evaluate the code walks.

Ben has allocated a staff-only git repository for code-walk grades and feedback:

    github.ccs.neu.edu/CS4500-F20/code-walk/

Before every lecture, Ben will place forms into three sub-directories of this repository and thus assign roles among students. For example, he may create

    Suzanne/

            9-18/

                 1-presenter1.txt

                 1-presenter2.txt

                 1-head.txt

                 1-assistant.txt

                 1-secretary.txt

                 ...

    

                 3-presenter1.txt

                 3-presenter2.txt

                 3-head.txt

                 3-assistant.txt

                 3-secretary.txt

In lieu of n-presenter1.txt and n-presenter2.txt, Ben may instead use n-designer1.txt and n-designer2.txt for all n. These second file names will be used to indicate that a pair presents a design rather than code. Each pair will get to present both. If we change our mind, please git-move the respective files so that his script balances the selection process properly.

Ben will send an email with the same information to the section head (with CC to the instructor).

The job of the section TA is to

  1. Review the presentation assignment for her section. Speak up — or edit — if there is a conflict.

  2. Shortly before class, email the presenters and panelists:

    • Suzanne: try to email late (after 10pm) on the night before class

    • Julia and Kanika: try to email in the morning (6am to 11am) on the day of.

    The goal is that students don’t have time to prepare, but do have time to opt-out if there is an emergency — the instructor and the head TA will pick ad hoc replacements.

  3. After class, commit grades and comments to this repository. The form files will have this shape:

    Here is the format for a report:

        Name: John Doe

        Email: doe.j@northeastern.edu

        Grade: ok+ | ok | ok- | 0

        

        ... informal prose ...

Ben will collect the comments and email them to the students, with a cc to the section head.

The section TAs will send out grades for the presenters and panelists, right after the postmortem. The instructors will send grades for the secretary memos after evaluating them; they will CC Ben for record keeping.
