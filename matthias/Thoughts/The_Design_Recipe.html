Where does the design recipe come from

part 1: the process is more important than the final result (G. Dopfer)

Since I get the impression that you know more Racket than a novice student, here’s a 30,000 ft-view of the story:

 

— we taught with plain Racket way way back (’91)

— we covered the whole seq. set of capabilities (functional, state/set!, control/callcc, and so on)

— on the final exam we asked for something as simple as !, a one-liner. A student turned in an entire page, using every capability of the language.

— we were pair-teaching and the student complained to my colleague about not getting points, challenging him to find a counter example.

— my colleague typed in the code .. and couldn’t come up with a counter example.

— I walked in. I took one look and said “it has nothing to do with correctness. If anybody is supposed to add a feature to this code, he will have to throw it away. It’s incomprehensible”

 

Two ingredients pushed me to tell students they had to use the design recipe:

 

        — I was teaching portions of it (what I call template now) and another colleague made a remark that caused me to broadened it to most of what we see now

        — My math teacher in high school never graded the __result__ of a problem.If the __process__ was good, you got an 100 points, no matter how wrong the numeric result was.

                I wanted to achieve the same thing in Programming.
