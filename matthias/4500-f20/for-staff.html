Roles

Ben

Ben Greenman will manage the github accounts for the students in the three sections, run the test fests, and collect the grades (automatically from the test fests and grade reports sent to students).

Each homework requires the following actions:

  • on the morning after the due date, push out the self evaluation for the assignment (if applicable);

  • also on the morning, harvest the solutions from the pairs;

  • and check the "delivery" and "executable" part of the test fest.

  • Start running the test fest.

  • 24 hours later, git-pull just the self evaluations and assign each head and all graders a certain number of github accounts to grade.

  • Ideally each pair of students should get results from the test fest within 48 to 72 hours in a directory in the specific homework directory.

The head of each section will assist the instructor with conducting code walks: managing questions from on-line participants, taking notes, making suggestions, and correcting the instructor (if needed). The shoulder attends too and "shoulders" some of the work of the head. After the lecture, the instructor and shoulder meet and evaluate the code walks.

Once homework solutions are assigned to a section, the heads will meet with their graders and jointly sample-grade some solutions. The goal is for the team to develop a common interpretation of the rubric. The section heads will communicate with each other and MF to resolve ambiguities.

Graders should bring up any difficulties with their section heads first, including interpreting the rubric, grading, meeting with students, or being late with grading.

Once the graders have evaluated the students’ work, the section heads will go check a selection from each grader to enforce a reasonable degree of consistency.
